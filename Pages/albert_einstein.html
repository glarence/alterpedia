Albert Einstein

Born: March 14, 1879

Died: April 18, 1955

Nationality: German

Residence: Germany, Italy, Switzerland, Austria (today: Czech Republic), Belgium, United States

Fields: Physics, philosophy

College: ETH Zurich, University of Zurich

Spouse: Elsa Lowenthal (1919-1936)

Children: 3
Awards
  • Barnard Medal (20)
  • Nobel Prize in Physics (21)
  • Matteucci Medal (21)
  • ForMemRS (21)
  • Copley Medal (25)
  • Max Planck Medal (29)
  • Time Person of the Century (99)

Albert Einstein was a German-born theoretical physicist.

Einstein was regarded as one of the smartest intellectuals of all time, thanks to many of his discoveries

He made numerous groundbreaking discoveries in the science field and remains as one of the most important people of all time.

What Could've Been

We would have missed many scientific discoveries, like the general theory of relativity, the photoelectric effect, and more

The US would not have been warned about the development of atomic bombs by Nazi Germany, leading to the Manhattan Project that ended WWII

There would not be 30,000 documents of scientific research

"Einstein" would not be associated with genius in pop culture
