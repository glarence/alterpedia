Genres: Rock, pop

  • Paul McCartney
  • George Harrison
  • Ringo Starr

The Beatles were an English rock band, formed in Liverpool in 1960.

With members John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison and Ringo Starr, they became widely regarded as the foremost and most influential act of the rock era.

They were one of the best things to happen in the twentieth century, let alone the Sixties.

What Could've Been

600 million records would not have been bought, making The Beatles one of the best selling artists of all time

We would be missing one of the most influential artists of all time, who introduced a shift from American artists' global dominance of rock and roll to British acts, the proliferation of young musicians in the 1960s who formed new bands, the term "Beatlesque" used to describe similar-sounding artists

The album may not have become the dominant form of record consumption over singles as it was the previous decade before streaming

No "Beatlemania", the intense fan frenzy directed toward them during the early years of their success, which was the precedent before today's fanboyism for certain artists
