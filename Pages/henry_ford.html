Henry Ford

Born: July 30, 1863

Died: April 7, 1947

Occupation: business magnate, engineering

Nationality: American

College: Goldsmith, Bryant & Stratton Business College

Title
  • Ford Motor (Founder)

Spouse: Clara Jane Bryant (m. 1888)

Children: 1

Religion: Anglican
Awards
  • Franklin Institute's Elliott Cresson Medal (28)
  • Grand Cross of the German Eagle (38)
  • Gallup's List of Widely Admired People of the 20th Century (99)

Henry Fordwas an American industrialist, the founder of the Ford Motor Company, and the sponsor of the development of the assembly line technique of mass production.

He revolutionized the automobile industry and led innovations in mass production.

Ford also had many business innovations like his franchising system of dealerships.

What Could've Been

Cars would not be as readily available or affordable as they are now without Henry Ford's innovations in mass production

No Henry Ford means no Fordism: mass production of inexpensive goods coupled with high wages for workers

There may be no system of dealerships, which also helped lead to lower prices for cars
