Martin Luther King

Born: January 15, 1929

Died: April 4, 1968 (assassination)

Occupation: clergyman, activist

Nationality: American

Organization: Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC)

Movement: African-American Civil Rights Movement, Peace movement

College: Morehouse College, Crozer Theological Seminary, Boston University

Spouse: Coretta Scott King (m. 1953)

Martin Luther King Jr was an American Baptist minister, activist, humanitarian, and leader in the African-American Civil Rights Movement.

He is best known for his role in the advancement of civil rights using nonviolent civil disobedience based on his Christian beliefs.

He is also known as one of the greatest orators of all time.
