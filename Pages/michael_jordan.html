Michael Jordan

Born: February 17, 1963

Height: 6 ft 6 in Weight: 216 lb

College: North Carolina

Team:
  • Chicago Bulls(1984-1993, 1995-1998)
  • Washington Wizards(2001-2003)
Accolades and Records
  • 6x NBA Champion (1991-1993, 1996-1998)
  • 6x NBA Finals MVP (1991-1993, 1996-1998)
  • 5x NBA MVP (1988, 1991-1992, 1996, 1998)
  • 14x NBA All-Star (1985-1993, 1996-1998, 2002-2003)
  • 10x All-NBA First Team (1987-1993, 1996-1998)
  • NBA Defensive Player of the Year (1988)
  • 9x NBA All-Defensive First Team (1988-1993, 1996-1998)
  • NBA Rookie of the Year (1985)
  • 10x NBA scoring champion (1987-1993, 1996-1998)
  • 3x NBA steals champion (1988, 1990, 1993)/li>
  • NBA playoffs all-time leading scorer
  • Chicago Bulls all-time leading scorer
  • 1984 and 1992 Olympics Basketball Gold Medal

Michael Jordan is regarded by many current and former players, critics/analysts, and fans as one of the greatest, if not THE greatest, players of all time.

He was one of the most athletic, acrobatic guards and pioneered the movement of backcourt players who could impact the game as much as a traditional bigman, which much of his influence can be seen today.

As a global icon and now a living legend, Michael Jordan helped turn basketball into one of the most popular sports in the world to watch and play.

What Could've Been

Basketball may not be the 3rd most popular sport in the world or 2nd most popular in the US

NBA would not be one of the most global sports leagues in the world, with 215 countries tuning into the 2015 NBA Finals

The Chicago Bulls would be without 6 NBA titles, their only NBA titles to date, which ranks them as the franchise with the 3rd most championships

NBA fashion would be stuck with plain white sneakers and short shorts as Jordan led the change of modern sportswear and flashy sneakers

Hall of Famers Reggie Miller, Patrick Ewing, John Stockton, Karl Malone, Charles Barkley, and more would've won a ring if it weren't for "His Airness"
