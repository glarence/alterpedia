Roger Federer is seen as one of the greatest tennis players of all time, if not the greatest, by many commentators, players, and fans.

Federer's beautifully graceful game has swooned audiences for almost two decades, gaining one of the largest, and most fervent fanbases of any sportsman

An extremely talented athlete, his all-rounded game pushed the game to new limits, leading one of the most dominant eras for tennis ever.

What Could've Been

There may be no "Golden Era" of tennis without Federer, who started and carried it with his massive haul of 17 majors, the most of all time, 24 Masters 1000s, 3rd most of all time, and 88 career titles, ranking for 3rd in the open era

Rival Roddick may have won more majors than his lone 2003 US Open title, with 3 Wimbledon losses to Federer alone

Brands like Nike, Rolex, Mercedes Benz, Moët & Chandon, and more would not have one of the most marketable ambassadors to carry their brand

Still playing at the age of 34, Federer has led the change in longevity, the average age of the top 15 being 28.6 years, up from 23.2 in 1992

Someone else would have at least one ATP Fan's Favorite Award, with Federer winning the last eleven years
