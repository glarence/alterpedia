Rosa Parks

Born: February 4, 1913

Died: October 24, 2005

Occupation: Civil rights activist

Nationality: American

Home town: Tuskegee, Alabama

Spouse: Raymond Parks (1932-1977)
Accolades and Records
  • NAACP Spingarn Medal (79)
  • Martin Luther King Jr Award (80)
  • michigan Women's Hall of Fame (83)
  • Peace Abbey Courage of Conscience Award (92)
  • Academy of Achievement's Golden Plate Award (95)
  • Presidential Medal of Freedom (96)
  • Congressional Gold Medal (99)
  • Alabama Academy of Honor (00)

Rosa Parks was an African-American Civil Rights activist, whom the United States Congress called "the first lady of civil rights" and "the mother of the freedom movement".

Her birthday, February 4, and the day she was arrested, December 1, have both become Rosa Parks Day, commemorated in both California and Ohio.

Parks' act of defiance and the Montgomery Bus Boycott became important symbols of the modern Civil Rights Movement.

She became an international icon of resistance to racial segregation.
